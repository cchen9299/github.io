NaS batteries are constructed from liquid sodium and sulfur. They have high energy density, high efficiency, and a long life cycle.

AEP conducted the first demonstration of a NaS battery in the United States.

Three 2 MW/14/4 MWh NaS batteries are developed that feature peak load shaving, which increases reliability by providing backup power to Milton, West Virginia; Churubusco, Indiana; and Bluffton, Ohio. This innovation reduces strain on the power grid as a whole.

4 MW/24 MWh NaS batteries provide sufficient backup power to allow for a new transmission line to be built from Marfa, Texas to Presidio, Texas, without inconvenience to residents.

A NaS battery system goes online to back up Appalachian Power Company’s distribution network.

AEP invests $5 million in Greensmith, a company specializing in energy storage software and integration services. AEP also partners with Greensmith on a 2 MW/14 MWh energy storage system in West Virginia.
