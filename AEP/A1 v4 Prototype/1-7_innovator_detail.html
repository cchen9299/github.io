JAMES MARKOWSKY

EXECUTIVE VICE PRESIDENT OF POWER GENERATION (1996-2000)



THE CREATIVE ENGINEER


Born to Ukrainian parents and raised in West Germany, James Markowsky earned a doctorate in mechanical engineering from Cornell University. He was named senior engineer at AEP at the age of 31.


Markowsky led a research and development task force that drove nearly 100 innovations, including the creation of pressurized fluidized bed combustion (PFBC), which is the process of removing sulfur during the combustion of coal, significantly reducing emissions. He championed the use of PFBC and other environmentally conscious technologies throughout his career.


Markowsky and fellow engineer Manoj Guha provided technical expertise when AEP converted the Zimmer Plant from nuclear to coal in the 1980s. The conversion showed the versatility of AEP’s power generation systems and the importance of creative engineering to protect company investments and provide power safely to consumers.


Markowsky became Assistant Secretary for Fossil Energy at the U.S. Department of Energy in 2009. In that role, he advised the federal government on the roles of coal, oil and natural gas in combating climate change.
