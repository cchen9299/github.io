HOWARD C. BARNES

ASSISTANT VICE PRESIDENT OF ENGINEERING (1968-1974)



THE SAFETY SCION


A graduate of Rose Polytechnic Institute, Howard Barnes joined American Gas and Electric (AGE) in 1939 as an engineer with Ohio Power Company.


Under the guidance of Philip Sporn, Barnes helped to develop the 765-kilovolt transmission lines and the extra high voltage (EHV) transmission system. He wrote more than 50 technical papers on power-system protection, solid-state circuits and digital relays.


Barnes’ research on the physical and medical effects of alternating current electric-field effects led AEP to implement important safety measures for its field technicians. His work was also influential in contemporary studies of exposure to alternating current with cellphones and other wireless devices.


In 1968, he became assistant vice president of engineering, holding that position until his retirement. Barnes’ commitment to engineering helped form AEP’s identity as not only a utility company but a research agency that created better and safer systems.
