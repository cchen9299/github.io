PHILIP SPORN

PRESIDENT AND CEO OF AMERICAN GAS AND ELECTRIC (1947-1961)



THE PRACTICAL SCHOLAR


Born in Austria, Philip Sporn joined American Gas and Electric (AGE) as an engineer in 1920. He rose steadily within the company, becoming chief electrical engineer in 1927, chief engineer in 1933, and vice president of engineering in 1934.


Sporn pioneered new methods of energy efficiency, turbine technology, high-voltage transmission and plant expansion. His interest in these innovations was so strong that he conducted a number of studies and wrote more than 100 papers and 10 books over the course of his career. In 1947, he became president of AGE.


In the 1950s, Sporn created the East Central Nuclear Group (ECNG) and the Nuclear Power Group to research and develop nuclear energy. He organized the Nuclear Power Group, which worked with Commonwealth Edison to design and build Dresden III in Morris, Illinois, one of the first nuclear power stations. 


Sporn’s skills as an engineer and manager enabled AEP to not only grow but also become an industry leader in innovation, technology, and safety.
