E. L INN DRAPER JR.

PRESIDENT (1992-2004)



THE NEXT-GEN NAVIGATOR


Before joining AEP, E. Linn Draper Jr. had a distinguished career in nuclear energy and the utilities industry.


Draper graduated from Rice and Cornell and worked as a professor and director of the nuclear engineering program at the University of Texas. He then spent nearly a decade as a nuclear technician and senior manager at Gulf States Utilities.


In 1992, he became president of AEP and in 1993 was appointed chairman, president and CEO.


Draper’s background in nuclear energy helped AEP navigate a series of political and economic shifts in the ’90s and 2000s. In 1992, the Energy Policy Act mandated that utility companies increase their use of clean energy. In response, Draper led AEP’s development of solar and wind power generation stations and the closing of coal plants.


One of Draper’s most notable achievements was the 1997 merger of AEP and Central and South West (CSW). Draper worked tirelessly to ensure a smooth transition that would create a company with diverse fuel and generation capabilities that stretched from Mexico to Canada.
