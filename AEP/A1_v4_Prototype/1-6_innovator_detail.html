DONALD C. COOK

AEP CHAIRMAN (1971-1976)



THE BIG-PICTURE THINKER


Before he became president of AEP in 1961, Donald C. Cook served as chairman of the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission.


At AEP, Cook focused on finding better, faster and more efficient ways to produce and distribute electricity, relying on the expertise of people both within AEP and abroad.


During his tenure, Cook’s no-nonsense management style helped the company navigate the tumultuous 1960s and 1970s, including the oil embargos, rising inflation, pushback from the environmental movement, and rising electrical costs.


Always ahead of consumer demand, Cook utilized all available energy resources (fossil fuel, nuclear, and hydroelectric power) and oversaw massive growth of the system’s largest individual coal-fired generating units.


Under his leadership, AEP became the first eastern utility company to mine low-sulfur coal from the West and invest in a robust barge- and train-based delivery system. Cook also oversaw the development of the world’s highest-voltage transmission network and the construction of AEP’s first nuclear plant.
