JOHN E. DOLAN

VICE CHAIRMAN-ENGINEERING AND CONSTRUCTION (1979-1988)



THE MIRACLE WORKER


A graduate of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and Columbia University, John Dolan worked on Philip Sporn’s project to develop a desalination and power plant in Eliat, Israel, in 1961.


Together, Dolan and Sporn made AEP’s first international project a success. Dolan noted that the plant not only improved people’s lives but also helped promote tourism in Israel.


The experience propelled Dolan’s rise through the company’s engineering leadership. He was named vice chairman in 1979 and held that position until his retirement in 1988.


Dolan oversaw the development and implementation of many of the innovations of the Sporn era, including seven units that generated 1.3 million kilowatts each.


In 1976, Dolan helped develop the Cook Nuclear Plant, the first nuclear plant in the AEP system, and he proposed the groundbreaking conversion of the Zimmer Plant from nuclear to coal in the 1980s.


The John E. Dolan Engineering Laboratory, established in 1987, is a tribute to his efforts to develop safe and technologically advanced energy sources at the dawn of the nuclear age.
