HORIZONTAL AXIS

Most horizontal-axis turbines have either two or three blades, operating “upwind,” or with the blades facing the wind. AEP’s wind farms use this type of turbine.                

VERTICAL AXIS

Vertical-axis turbines, like the one pictured here, have two horizontal blades around a vertical shaft, or airfoil. They use the same physical principles that give an airplane “lift”: Air passing over the blades is converted into rotational momentum, which spins the generator, thus creating electricity.
